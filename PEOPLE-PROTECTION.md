# PEOPLE-PROTECTION.md

## Purpose

People Protection is a non-optional JPV-OS governance layer. It defines the minimum human-safety, dignity, autonomy, consent, anti-exploitation, and anti-discrimination requirements for JayPVentures LLC systems, creator surfaces, automation, AI behavior, monetization flows, research programs, partnerships, and platform integrations.

No system may be considered production-ready if it protects infrastructure while leaving people exposed.

This policy exists because technical excellence without human protection is incomplete. Infrastructure can be fast, automated, secure, and scalable while still harming people through coercive design, unsafe dependency, opaque decisions, discriminatory outcomes, surveillance, exclusion, or exploitative monetization. JPV-OS rejects that failure mode by treating people protection as a required production gate, not a secondary ethics statement.

## Philosophical Foundation

JPV-OS is built on the position that systems inherit moral responsibility from the effects they create. A system is not neutral merely because it is automated, profitable, efficient, or technically secure. If it affects access, identity, money, opportunity, safety, education, reputation, labor, or civic participation, it must be evaluated by how it treats the people subject to it.

The governing philosophy is simple: people are not inputs to be optimized away. People are the reason infrastructure exists.

This policy is grounded in six principles:

1. Human dignity is non-negotiable. No person may be reduced to a metric, entitlement state, risk score, behavioral profile, monetization target, or automation object.
2. Autonomy requires meaningful choice. Consent is invalid when users are trapped, misled, coerced, surveilled, manipulated, or denied a reasonable way to understand consequences.
3. Power must be accountable. Any system capable of denying access, shaping opportunity, routing money, moderating participation, or escalating enforcement must be explainable and reviewable.
4. Safety includes social and economic harm. Security is not limited to preventing technical compromise; it also includes preventing exploitation, discrimination, retaliation, dependency abuse, and institutional misuse.
5. Automation must not erase responsibility. AI and workflow systems may assist decisions, but they must not become a shield that hides who is accountable.
6. Growth is not a justification for harm. Monetization, scale, partner demands, investor pressure, operational convenience, or speed may not override human protection.

## Authority

This policy applies to:

- JayPVentures LLC enterprise infrastructure
- jaypventures creator and community systems
- jaypVLabs research and learning systems
- JPV-OS orchestration, entitlement, identity, and governance layers
- Cloudflare Workers, GitHub workflows, Microsoft/Azure integrations, Stripe/Discord/Wix workflows, and any future automation or AI-assisted process
- public websites, dashboards, portals, intake forms, checkout flows, entitlement systems, community systems, internal tooling, and deployment workflows

People Protection overrides convenience, growth pressure, monetization pressure, partner demands, and automation shortcuts.

## Core Standard

Every JPV-OS-aligned system must preserve:

- human dignity
- informed consent
- user autonomy
- equal treatment
- accessibility
- explainability
- reversible or appealable decisions where materially impactful
- privacy and data minimization
- protection from coercion, exploitation, manipulation, and unsafe dependency
- fair remediation when a system error causes harm
- accountable human review for high-impact decisions

## Protected People and Affected Groups

This policy protects all people affected by JPV-OS-aligned systems, including:

- customers and members
- creators and community participants
- workers, contractors, collaborators, and vendors
- students, learners, and research participants
- administrators and operators
- applicants, prospects, and leads
- people indirectly affected by automated decisions, recommendations, routing, or enforcement
- vulnerable users, economically pressured users, minors where an approved student program exists, and people affected by accessibility barriers

Protection does not depend on whether a person is paying, logged in, technically sophisticated, or formally under contract.

## Prohibited Uses

The following are permanently prohibited:

- racism, caste systems, slavery, forced labor, human ownership, or dehumanizing treatment
- systems that normalize discrimination, exclusion, exploitation, or unequal access based on protected identity traits
- unlawful surveillance or covert monitoring
- social scoring, coercive behavioral profiling, or automated punishment systems
- coercive biometric tracking or involuntary identity capture
- political suppression, viewpoint punishment, or civic intimidation
- autonomous targeting of harm
- manipulative AI behavior designed to remove meaningful choice
- dark patterns that trap users into subscriptions, dependency, payment, or platform lock-in
- exploitation of creators, workers, students, children, vulnerable users, or economically pressured communities
- institutional, government, partner, investor, administrator, or internal abuse of emergency authority
- retaliation against users or contributors for asking questions, appealing decisions, reporting harm, or refusing unreasonable data collection

## Consent, Notice, and Choice

Consent must be informed, specific, understandable, and revocable where the system allows revocation. A person must not be forced into broad consent for unrelated processing, hidden profiling, unrelated AI training, unrelated marketing, or avoidable data exposure.

Systems must provide clear notice when they collect personal data, make material automated decisions, route users into paid access, assign roles, change entitlements, restrict participation, or escalate a user to review or enforcement.

Choice is not meaningful when the only alternative is exclusion from necessary access, unreasonable friction, hidden cancellation barriers, or unexplained denial.

## Privacy and Data Minimization

JPV-OS-aligned systems must collect the minimum data required for the stated purpose. Data collection must have a defined purpose, retention expectation, access boundary, and deletion or archival pathway.

Systems must avoid persistent identifiers, behavioral tracking, profiling, or cross-context data linkage unless there is a documented need, a valid user-facing purpose, and appropriate safeguards.

Public Mode systems must remain especially strict: stateless where possible, no user-level profiling by default, aggregate metrics only, and no persistent identifiers unless a clear access or safety need exists.

## AI and Automation Requirements

AI-assisted systems must support people without replacing accountability.

Material automated decisions must include:

- a clear decision reason
- a policy basis
- logging sufficient for review
- a human escalation path when the decision affects access, money, safety, identity, opportunity, education, or reputation
- rollback or appeal support where technically and operationally possible
- documented limits on model authority
- a fail-safe path when confidence is low, context is missing, or the decision could materially harm a person

AI systems must not be used to disguise accountability, manufacture consent, automate exploitation, or pressure people into choices they would not reasonably make with clear information.

AI-generated content, recommendations, scoring, routing, moderation, entitlement decisions, and support responses must remain subject to review standards appropriate to their risk level.

## Creator Protection

Creator-facing systems must protect creators from hidden dependency models, coercive monetization, forced exclusivity, unfair role removal, opaque payment routing, unexplained access loss, and sudden rule changes without notice or remediation.

Creator access, community roles, monetization benefits, payout routing, VIP status, and membership privileges must be explainable. When access is denied, removed, downgraded, or corrected, the system must preserve a record of the reason, source event, policy basis, and available support path.

JPV-OS must not create creator economies that depend on trapping creators inside one platform, one vendor, one payment path, one algorithmic surface, or one opaque entitlement authority.

## Worker and Economic Protection

Worker-facing or automation-supported systems must not treat workforce displacement as an acceptable default outcome. Automation must be paired with transition logic, human review, retraining pathways, operational transparency, and harm-reduction planning when people are materially affected.

Automation may reduce repetitive work, improve consistency, and increase operational reach. It must not be used to hide wage suppression, unsafe labor expectations, arbitrary termination, discriminatory ranking, or denial of opportunity without review.

Where AI changes duties, evaluation, scheduling, compensation, access, or role expectations, the system owner must identify foreseeable harms and define mitigation before production use.

## Child and Student Protection

JPV-OS systems are 18+ by default unless a separate child or student program is intentionally created.

Any child or student program must be isolated from:

- behavioral advertising
- unrelated monetization
- unrelated AI training
- exploitative engagement loops
- data resale
- non-educational profiling
- adult commercial funnels
- unnecessary direct messaging or community exposure

Child or student programs require guardian, institutional, or legally appropriate consent; privacy-by-default design; accessibility protections; limited retention; purpose limitation; age-appropriate explanations; safety review; and strict separation from adult commercial systems.

Education systems must prioritize learning, safety, accessibility, curiosity, dignity, and institutional accountability. Students must not be treated as data inventory, behavioral products, or monetization targets.

## Accessibility and Inclusion

People Protection requires accessible systems. Public and member-facing systems must be designed so that disability, device limitations, language barriers, cognitive load, financial pressure, or unfamiliarity with technical systems do not become hidden exclusion mechanisms.

Design reviews should account for readable language, visible support paths, keyboard and screen-reader compatibility where applicable, mobile usability, color contrast, error clarity, and plain-language explanations for important decisions.

## Institutional and Government Misuse Limits

No JPV-OS-aligned system may be used to enable unlawful surveillance, civic suppression, discriminatory enforcement, coercive biometric tracking, autonomous harm targeting, political intimidation, emergency-power overreach, or social scoring.

Rights-preserving constraints may not be removed through acquisition, merger, investor influence, executive transition, partner demand, or policy drift.

Emergency authority must be narrow, logged, time-bound, reviewable, and limited to legitimate safety or continuity needs. Emergency authority may not become a permanent bypass of rights, privacy, appeal, or accountability.

## Monetization Boundaries

Revenue systems must not depend on coercion, deception, forced exclusivity, artificial scarcity, manipulative pricing, hidden cancellation barriers, or exploitative access design.

Subscription, entitlement, payment, and creator-access systems must provide clear terms, clear status, explainable denials, support channels, and fair remediation paths.

Pricing, upgrades, trials, VIP access, checkout flows, cancellation flows, and renewal flows must be designed around informed choice, not confusion or pressure.

## Enforcement Requirements

Before any production promotion, the responsible operator must verify that the system:

1. identifies who could be harmed;
2. identifies how harm could occur;
3. documents mitigation controls;
4. defines appeal, support, rollback, or remediation paths;
5. logs material decisions;
6. avoids unnecessary personal data collection;
7. avoids discriminatory, coercive, or exploitative patterns;
8. keeps child/student systems isolated when applicable;
9. preserves human oversight for high-impact decisions;
10. can be audited against this policy;
11. provides clear user-facing notice where decisions affect access, payment, identity, entitlement, safety, or opportunity;
12. documents whether the system is public, internal, creator-facing, enterprise-facing, research-facing, or student-facing;
13. prevents administrator, partner, or emergency bypass from becoming unlogged or permanent;
14. includes a remediation path for system error, false denial, wrongful downgrade, or unsafe automation outcome.

## People Protection Review Questions

Every production review must answer:

- Who is affected by this system?
- What power does the system have over them?
- What could go wrong if the system is incorrect, biased, misused, compromised, or misunderstood?
- What data is collected, and why is each category necessary?
- Can the person understand the decision or workflow?
- Can the person appeal, correct, cancel, or request support?
- Could this system pressure someone into payment, dependency, disclosure, or silence?
- Could this system be misused by an administrator, partner, institution, or government actor?
- Does the system protect creators, workers, students, and vulnerable users differently where their risk is higher?
- What logs prove that the system followed this policy?

## Evidence Required for Production Readiness

A production-ready system must be able to show evidence of:

- documented purpose and affected-user analysis
- privacy and data minimization review
- entitlement or access decision reason codes where applicable
- support, appeal, rollback, or remediation path
- administrator action logging where applicable
- child/student isolation review where applicable
- AI authority limits where applicable
- anti-discrimination and anti-exploitation review
- deployment or branch review confirming this policy was checked

## Relationship to Other Policies

This policy is binding alongside `GOVERNANCE.md` and `SECURITY.md`.

Security protects systems from compromise.
Governance protects decision integrity.
People Protection protects the humans affected by those systems.

All three are required for production readiness.

## Review Standard

This policy must be reviewed whenever a system adds or materially changes:

- identity or entitlement decisions
- payment, subscription, or access rules
- AI-assisted decision-making
- creator or worker workflows
- student or educational workflows
- data collection, retention, or sharing
- partner, institutional, or government-facing functionality
- emergency access, override, or escalation logic
- community moderation or role assignment logic
- public claims, trust pages, or safety representations

## Production Gate

A system may not be promoted as production-ready unless People Protection review is complete, documented, and enforceable through repository policy, workflow checks, or deployment review.

The production standard is not merely that a system works. The standard is that it works without sacrificing the people it affects.
